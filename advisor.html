What Is a Financial Advisor?

Financial advisor is a broad term that covers various professions such as brokers, financial planners, investment planners, consultants, tax planners, portfolio managers, and others. These professionals provide financial advisory services to help individuals manage their finances efficiently. A certified financial advisor is well-versed in various aspects financial planning in India, including budgeting, investing, retirement planning, debt management, tax saving, risk management, and more.

However, their area of expertise can vary based on their qualifications, experience, and specialisation. For example, a tax planner is considered a qualified financial advisor, but not all financial advisors are necessarily tax planners.

Similarly, a financial planner is a type of financial consultant who specialises in helping people achieve financial success by creating personalised strategies based on their risk tolerance, financial situation, and goals. A certified financial planner can help you create a detailed investment plan that ensures a peaceful retirement, or a comprehensive tax plan that ensures you save as much of your hard-earned money as possible.

How to Become a Certified Financial Advisor/Consultant?

There are different certifications available for different types of financial advisors. For example, the Chartered Financial Analyst or CFA is a certification for financial analysts, and Certified Financial Planner or CFP for financial planners.

Steps to becoming certified financial planner(CFP):

1

Education Requirement

Hold a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution

2

Experience

At least 1 year supervised or 3 years unsupervised relevant experience

3

Coursework

Complete CFP coursework approved by FPSB covering essential financial planning topics

4

Examination

Pass the CFP exam which is MCQ-based

5

Ethics & Verification

Sign ethics declaration and undergo verification process

Certified Financial Advisors vs Planners vs Consultants

Certified Financial Advisor

  • Offers variety of services - broader scope
  • May or may not be certified
  • General financial advice and referrals
  • Like a general surgeon - addresses many issues

Certified Financial Planner

  • Focus on short, mid, and long-term financial goals
  • Can be certified (CFPs with FPSB certification)
  • Detailed financial planning approach
  • Comprehensive goal-achievement strategies

Certified Financial Consultant

  • Highly specialised expert guidance
  • May or may not be certified
  • Short-term focused solutions
  • Specific area expertise

Key Certifications to Look For

Certified Financial Planner (CFP)

Comprehensive service including investment planning, tax optimization, risk protection, emergency planning, debt management, retirement services, and estate planning.

Chartered Wealth Manager (CWM)

Services for high-net-worth individuals, managing wealth and estate for affluent clients.

Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA)

Investment management experts who manage portfolios and offer personalized investment advice.

NISM Series V-A

Mandatory qualification for mutual fund distributors, demonstrating thorough understanding of mutual fund products and regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a digital financial planner do?

Digital financial planners use technology and AI to provide automated financial advice, portfolio management, and planning services through online platforms and mobile apps.
